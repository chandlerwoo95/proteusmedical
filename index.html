Foreign body ingenstion is a top ten veterinary emergency that requires immediate care to remove the objects from the animal's gastrointestinal system.

Dogs between 40-60 lbs are the most common offenders, often consuming items such as fruit pits, coins, glass clumps, and tennis balls.

Endoscopic foreign body retrieval is a minimally-invasive procedure to extract these foreign objects, as opposed to surgical options that are expensive and timely.

Streamlining the retrieval device is a veterinary priority to increase both the efficacy of the retrieval process while minimizing harm for the patient.
