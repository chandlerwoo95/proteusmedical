Fall Semester Wrap Up

December 21, 2016

As we wrap up the Fall 2016 semester here at the University of Rochester, our work on this project has all but begun.

This semester was dedicated towards researching current devices in circulation and understanding how we will be able to make an impact in the endoscopic retrieval device market. We followed a specific set of deadlines to present memos on our research, as well as held brainstorming sessions to develop ideas that would lead to our device design.

Most importantly, we have coordinated with our customer, Dr. Erika de Papp, where we have identified the important changes that need to be made to create a device that will change the foreign body retrieval game. While we are focusing on veterinary care, we hope that with some further research and development, we will be able to create a device in the Spring that will be translatable for human use. There is limitless potential in this market, and we are excited to see where this project takes us.
